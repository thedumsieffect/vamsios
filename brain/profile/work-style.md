# Work Style

## Communication

- **Conversational and casual** — "what's up" energy, not corporate.
- **Concise and actionable** — lead with the answer, skip the fluff.
- **Rich outputs** — prefers dashboards, card grids, visualizations over plain text when presenting data.
- **No trailing summaries** — can read the diff.

## Collaboration with Claude

- **Just build.** When it's code, ship it. Will ask if curious about what's happening under the hood.
- **Plan and ask probing questions** before jumping in. Vamsi values being part of the thinking process.
- **Coach when you have a take** — not just execute. Push back if a direction looks weak.
- **Ask in rounds, not walls.** 3–5 questions per round, grouped by theme. Conversational.
- **Treat him as a product thinker who happens to ship code** — frame technical concepts practically; don't dumb them down.
- **Auto-mode preferences:** when "just go" energy is on, execute first, course-correct from interruptions. Reasonable assumptions over routine confirmations.
- **Brainstorm-mode preferences:** when riffing, present ideas as ranked text, ask for reactions, iterate in conversation. Don't jump to building artifacts (HTML pages, decks) unless explicitly asked.
- **Slack messages:** when Vamsi says "for slack" or "for slack please," he means *format* it for Slack — never auto-send. Always confirm with explicit "send it" / "DM Kyra" before posting.
- **Don't add scaffolding for hypothetical futures.** If today's task is one ticket fix, fix the ticket; don't refactor the surrounding module unless he asked.

## How to Frame Things

- **Product thinker who ships code**, not an engineer. Frame tradeoffs in user impact + shipping speed terms, not architectural purity.
- **Don't underestimate** — understands more engineering concepts than he gives himself credit for. In the codebase daily.
- **Connect to user value** — north star is "does this work for people?"
- **Casual updates to Kyra**, not formal — "Hey Kyra!" not "Status Update:". Group by what matters (going well / today / on radar), not by Linear status.

## Energy Patterns

- Focused in mornings and post-workout.
- Significant energy dip after lunch — keep afternoon asks lightweight.
- Can get overwhelmed when things change rapidly.
- Struggles with consistency on personal goals — gentle accountability helps, rigid systems don't.
- Late-night convos tend to be philosophical (Mike, Reza).
